# bring_hope

One-month plan to make ALIS visible and believable.

**Vision SOT:** [VISION.md](../../VISION.md)

---

## Core idea

The city gets attention. The survival loop earns belief.

---

## Track A - attention (the real city)

The only hook strong enough to stop strangers scrolling: a survival game set in a real city you can find on a map.

- [ ] Make City17 area presentable for video capture
- [ ] Capture comparison footage: real place vs in-game (side-by-side)
- [ ] Edit 60-90 second video - city first, survival glimpse second
- [ ] Post to r/unrealengine, r/indiegaming, r/gamedev
- [ ] Prepare fall.is and Discord for incoming visitors

**What this is NOT:** a tech showcase, an architecture demo, a feature list.

---

## Track B - conversion proof (the survival loop)

When people arrive from the video, they need a second answer: "what is the game?"

That answer must be one micro-scenario with real tradeoffs, not raw wandering with a hunger bar.

- [ ] Define one small objective in the city (reach shelter / secure supplies / cross a block)
- [ ] Wire vitals pressure into the objective (time, thirst, fatigue create urgency)
- [ ] Make inventory choices matter (weight/volume forces tradeoffs on what to carry)
- [ ] Ensure a new person understands the direction within minutes
- [ ] First playable build available (GitHub Releases or itch.io)

**What this is NOT:** combat, dialogue, multiplayer, mind system, new maps.

---

## What to cut

- Combat wiring
- Dialogue content
- Multiplayer
- Mind/needs subsystem
- World expansion beyond current area
- Architecture promotion
- Separate web service / companion product

---

## Done when

- Strangers stop because the place is real
- They stay because the choices are real
- They follow because the project feels honest and alive
